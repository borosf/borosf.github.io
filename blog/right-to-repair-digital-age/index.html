Right to Repair in Digital Age

January 10, 2024

Why device longevity matters more than ever in our digital economy, and how the right to repair movement is shaping sustainable technology practices.

Designing products that are serviceable and repair-friendly reduces e‑waste, extends lifecycles, and gives users true ownership. From accessible documentation to spare-part availability, right to repair principles support both sustainability and consumer rights.

Policy momentum is growing globally. Clear standards around diagnostics, schematics, and firmware updates can unlock competitive repair markets while maintaining safety and security. This aligns with broader goals around circular economy and responsible design.

Developers and manufacturers can help by choosing interoperable components, avoiding unnecessary DRM, and publishing repair guides. In the long run, everyone benefits from tech that’s built to last and easy to fix.
